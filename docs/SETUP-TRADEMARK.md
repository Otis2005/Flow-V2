# Registering TenderFlow as a trademark

Practical, Kenya-first guide to making "TenderFlow" legally yours. This is informational; for binding legal advice consult a Kenyan IP attorney before you sign anything.

---

## What a trademark gets you

- **Exclusive right** to use "TenderFlow" in the registered classes within the registered countries.
- **Standing to sue** copycats who pass off as your brand.
- **The ® symbol** (only after registration completes; before that, you can use ™ which is informal and unregistered).
- **Asset value**: the registered mark is a transferable asset that licensees or acquirers will pay for.
- **Domain disputes**: WIPO and KENIC's dispute panels take registered trademarks seriously when adjudicating domain conflicts.

## What it does NOT get you

- Worldwide protection. Trademarks are country-by-country (or via international filing systems like Madrid Protocol).
- Protection of the logo automatically. Logos and word marks are filed separately; both should be registered for full protection.
- Protection of features, business model, or code. Those are patent, copyright, and trade-secret territory.

## Step 1: Trademark availability search (do this BEFORE you spend any money)

You must confirm no one else has registered "TenderFlow" (or a confusingly similar mark) in the classes you'd file under.

### Option A — DIY search

1. Open https://www.kipi.go.ke/
2. Look for **"Trade Marks Search"** or **"E-services → IP Online → Search"** in the menu.
3. Search for `TENDERFLOW` and variants: `TENDER FLOW`, `TENDR FLOW`, `TENDERFLO`.
4. Filter by classes 9, 35, 42.
5. Note any hits: registration number, owner, class, status (registered / pending / abandoned).

The KIPI online search is sometimes flaky. If results don't load, try in a different browser or visit KIPI in person (Popo Road, off Mombasa Road, Nairobi — bring a notebook).

### Option B — paid search through an attorney

Most Kenyan IP attorneys offer a search-and-opinion service for KSh 5,000–15,000. They'll do a thorough search of registered + pending + similar marks and give a written opinion on registrability. Worth it if you want a real safety net before paying registration fees.

### What to look for

- ✅ No results → safe to file
- ⚠ Exact match in unrelated class (e.g., a food brand called "Tenderflow" in Class 29) → low risk, file anyway with attorney guidance
- 🚩 Exact or near-match in Class 9 / 35 / 42 → contact owner about coexistence, contest registrability, or rebrand
- 🚩 The mark is "TFlow" or "Tendrflow" in our classes → confusingly similar, get legal advice before proceeding

## Step 2: Decide which classes to file under

Kenya uses the international **Nice Classification** (45 classes). For TenderFlow's scope, the relevant classes are:

| Class | What it covers | Why TenderFlow needs it | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **9** | Downloadable software, mobile applications, electronic publications, computer programs | Essential. The TenderFlow web app counts as downloadable software in the eyes of trademark law. | Must file |
| **42** | Software-as-a-Service, online platforms, design and development of computer hardware and software, hosting services | Essential. SaaS positioning lives here. | Must file |
| **35** | Business services, advertising, business management, online marketplaces, business consultancy | Recommended. Covers the consultant marketplace, featured listings revenue, business intelligence positioning. | Strongly recommended |
| 41 | Education, training, publication of educational materials | Optional. Only if you publish training content / certifications. | Skip for now |
| 16 | Printed matter, publications | Optional. Only if you publish a magazine or printed reports. | Skip |

**Recommendation**: file Classes 9, 35, 42 from day one. Three classes = three fees, but adding a class later costs more in legal fees than filing them together upfront.

### Costs (KIPI, as of 2026)

KIPI publishes the official fee schedule at https://www.kipi.go.ke/fees. Approximate amounts for a Kenyan applicant:

| Item | Approx. fee |
|---|---|
| Trademark search (official) | KSh 1,000 per class |
| Application filing | KSh 4,000 per class |
| Examination | KSh 4,000 per class |
| Publication in Industrial Property Journal | KSh 3,000 per class |
| Certificate of registration | KSh 4,000 per class |
| **Total per class (no opposition)** | **~KSh 16,000** |
| Total for 3 classes (9 + 35 + 42) | **~KSh 48,000** |

These are the official government fees. Attorneys add their own service fee on top.

### Attorney fee guidance

| Approach | Total approximate cost (3 classes) | Time |
|---|---|---|
| DIY via KIPI | KSh 48,000–60,000 | You handle paperwork, you correspond with KIPI |
| Attorney-managed (mid-range firm) | KSh 80,000–150,000 | Attorney handles search, drafting, filing, examination responses |
| Top-tier IP firm | KSh 150,000–300,000 | Premium service, fastest response to objections |

**Honest take**: for a single brand registration with no obvious complications, attorney-managed mid-range is the sweet spot. DIY is doable but the examination phase is where attorneys earn their fee (responding to KIPI office actions is technical).

## Step 3: Documents you'll need before filing

Have these ready before you (or your attorney) start the application:

### About the applicant

- [ ] Full legal name of the applicant. If you're filing personally: Kennedy Nange (per Kenyan ID). If filing under a company: the company name as registered with the Registrar of Companies.
- [ ] National ID number (for individuals) or Company Registration number (for companies).
- [ ] Postal address + email + phone.
- [ ] KRA PIN (Kenya Revenue Authority).

### About the mark

- [ ] **Word mark** — type out exactly: `TENDERFLOW` (all caps is convention; case doesn't matter for protection but KIPI's database is uppercase).
- [ ] **Stylised / device mark** — submit the logo separately. Use `public/brand/logo.svg` exported to PNG at 800×800 (clear background, 300 DPI). Convert via cloudconvert.com.
- [ ] **Colour claim** — if you want the specific colours protected (navy + amber + teal + gold), state them with hex codes in the application. Otherwise the mark is registered as black-and-white and protection extends to any colour combination.
- [ ] **Description of goods/services per class**:

#### Class 9
> Downloadable computer software for accessing, searching, browsing and managing tender and procurement opportunities; mobile applications for tender notifications and bid management; downloadable electronic publications relating to procurement.

#### Class 35
> Provision of an online business directory of tender consultants and bid management professionals; business intelligence services; advertising and promotion of tender notices; online business management and procurement support services.

#### Class 42
> Software-as-a-service (SaaS) featuring software for aggregating, searching, classifying and managing tender and procurement opportunities; design and development of computer software for tender management; hosting of an online searchable database of tender opportunities; artificial intelligence services for the analysis of tender documents.

### Decision: file in your name or your company's name?

| File as | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **Kennedy Nange (individual)** | Simpler, faster filing | Mark is personally owned. Transfer to a future company later requires a separate "assignment" filing (extra cost, paperwork). |
| **A registered company** (TenderFlow Ltd or similar) | Mark belongs to the business from day one; easier later for investors, acquirers, partnerships. | Requires you to first register a Kenyan limited company with the Business Registration Service (~KSh 10,650 + a few days). |

**Strong recommendation**: register a company first, then file the trademark in the company's name. The extra week is worth it. Companies are registered at https://brs.go.ke or via the eCitizen portal.

## Step 4: File the application

### Path A — DIY via KIPI (cheapest, most work)

1. Visit https://www.kipi.go.ke/ → IP Online → register an applicant account.
2. Once logged in: **New Application → Trade Mark**.
3. Fill the form (one application per class):
   - Mark type: choose either "Word" or "Stylised" (or both — file both as separate applications for maximum protection).
   - Class number.
   - Goods/services description (use the wording in Step 3 above).
   - Applicant details.
   - Upload logo PNG if applying for the stylised/device mark.
4. Pay the filing fee via the KIPI portal (M-Pesa Paybill works).
5. Submit. You'll get a filing receipt with a TM number (e.g., `TM/2026/12345`).
6. KIPI examines within 3-6 months. They might issue an "office action" requesting clarification — respond within the deadline (usually 60 days) or the application lapses.
7. If accepted: published in the Industrial Property Journal. **60-day opposition period** during which third parties can challenge.
8. If no opposition (or opposition fails): registration certificate issued. Total time: 12-18 months from filing.

### Path B — Attorney-managed (recommended for non-IP-savvy applicants)

1. Identify 2-3 Kenyan IP attorneys (suggestions below).
2. Email them with: "I want to register the trademark TENDERFLOW in classes 9, 35 and 42 in Kenya. Please quote your full-service fee and estimated timeline."
3. Compare quotes. Pick the one that:
   - Itemises fees clearly (filing fees vs service fees)
   - Includes the trademark search in the price
   - Responds within 2-3 business days
   - Offers a fixed fee (not hourly) for the filing stage
4. Sign their engagement letter, pay the retainer, hand over the documents from Step 3.
5. They file, respond to KIPI's questions, monitor for opposition.
6. You receive the registration certificate when issued.

## Step 5: After registration

Things to do once the certificate arrives (probably 2027):

1. **Update `docs/BRAND.md`** with the registration number and class list (the "official record" line in section 7).
2. **Start using the ® symbol** wherever you previously used ™ — on the site, in press, on business cards.
3. **File with the relevant trade body** if applicable (e.g., the Kenya Association of Manufacturers for cross-licensing networks).
4. **Set a renewal reminder** for 9 years from registration. Trademarks last 10 years and renew indefinitely. Don't miss the renewal window or you lose the mark.
5. **Monitor for infringement**. KIPI doesn't enforce; you do. Set a Google Alert for `TenderFlow` (and "tender flow", "tendr flow") so you spot copycats early.

## Step 6: East Africa expansion

Once your Kenyan registration is in hand, expanding to Uganda and Tanzania:

### Option A — File separately in each country
- Uganda: URSB (Uganda Registration Services Bureau). Filing fees similar to Kenya. https://ursb.go.ug/
- Tanzania: BRELA (Business Registrations and Licensing Agency). https://www.brela.go.tz/
- Total: ~KSh 100,000 across both, 12-18 months each.

### Option B — Madrid Protocol via WIPO (recommended)
- Once you have a Kenyan registration (or pending application), you can file a single international application designating Uganda and Tanzania (both are Madrid members).
- Single filing fee + per-country designation fees. Usually cheaper than separate filings.
- Application via https://www.wipo.int/madrid/en/

**Strong recommendation**: file in Kenya first, then use Madrid Protocol for everything else.
